This is me.

Ashmit Gurey is a mathematics student at the Kerala School of Mathematics, Kozhikode, India. His research lies in the study of inverse problems and X-ray tomography, focusing on the Radon and fan-beam transforms and their applications in computerized tomography.

He completed his undergraduate studies in Mathematics and Computing at the Institute of Mathematics and Applications, Bhubaneswar, and is currently pursuing his Master's Thesis under the guidance of Prof. Subrahmanyam Gorthi.

When he's not doing mathematics, Ashmit enjoys reading, exploring creative ideas, and developing interactive academic tools. He's open to collaborations in imaging and applied analysis, and is always looking for new opportunities to learn and contribute.
