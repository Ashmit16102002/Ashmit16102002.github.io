Summer Programme 2025

June 16 – July 11, 2025
Contents: 1. Topics 2. How to Apply 3. Important Dates 4. Lectures 5. Special Lectures 6. Schedule of Lectures 7. Selection List

TIFR Centre for Applicable Mathematics is organizing a Summer Programme mainly targeted toward Master’s and entry‑level PhD students focusing on Analysis and related topics during June 16 – July 11, 2025. The programme will consist of ten 90‑minute lectures.

1. Topics

  • Patterns, Entropy and Gibbs measures – Nishant Chandgotia
  • Geometric Measure Theory – Debabrata Karmakar
  • Microlocal Analysis and Inverse Problems – Venky Krishnan
  • Introduction to Harmonic Measure & Geometric Function Theory – Atul Shekhar
  • Reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS): from theory to applications – Sreekar Vadlamani
  • Multiscale Problems in PDEs – M Vanninathan

The detailed lecture descriptions follow below.

4. Lectures (Detailed Descriptions)

Topic: Patterns, Entropy and Gibbs measures

Instructor: Nishant Chandgotia, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

Statistical physics attempts to explain natural physical phenomena like freezing and magnetism…

References:
  1. An Introduction to Information Theory – Cover & Thomas
  2. Equilibrium States in Ergodic Theory – Keller
  3. Thermodynamic Formalism – David Ruelle

Topic: Geometric Measure Theory

Instructor: Debabrata Karmakar, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

Review of Lebesgue & Hausdorff measures, covering theorems, Carathéodory measurability…

Topic: Microlocal Analysis and Inverse Problems

Instructor: Venky Krishnan, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

We review basics of microlocal analysis and its applications in imaging and tomography problems.

References:
  1. The Analysis of Linear Partial Differential Operators Vol 1 – Hörmander
  2. Microlocal Analysis for Differential Operators – Grigis & Sjöstrand

Topic: Introduction to Harmonic Measure & Geometric Function Theory

Instructor: Atul Shekhar, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

Jordan domains, Dirichlet problem, Carathéodory’s theorem, Green's functions, quasicircles, etc.

References:
  1. Harmonic Measure – Garnett & Marshall
  2. Boundary Behaviour of Conformal Maps – Pommerenke

Topic: Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS)

Instructor: Sreekar Vadlamani, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

Introduction to RKHS, examples, theory, and applications in statistics/machine learning.

References:
  1. An Introduction to the Theory of RKHS – Paulsen & Raghupathi
  2. Kernel Mean Embedding of Distributions – Muandet et al.

Topic: Multiscale Problems in PDEs

Instructor: M Vanninathan, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

From continuum modeling to homogenization: microstructures, Bloch wave method, etc.

References:
  1. A Bensoussan, J-L Lions & G Papanicolaou – Asymptotic Analysis for Periodic Structures (1978)
  2. L Tartar – The General Theory of Homogenization (2009)
  3. G Allaire – Shape Optimization by the Homogenization Method (2002)

5. Special Lectures

Gram matrices: connecting GPS triangulation, etc.

Speaker: Apoorva Khare, IISc Bangalore

Gram matrices in GPS, metric embeddings, and Heron’s formula. Download Slides (pdf)

Sobolev type inequalities and related problems.

Speaker: Sandeep K, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

An exploration into sharp constants, PDE applications, and inequality theory.

Distance between walks on graphs

Speaker: Nishant Chandgotia, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

Exploring adjacency of paths in graph walks and connections to group theory.

The probabilistic method in analysis

Speaker: Koushik Ramachandran, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

Applying Erdős’s probabilistic method to harmonic and complex analysis.

Ballot problem, Young tableaux and Non-intersecting paths

Speaker: Manjunath Krishnapur, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

Counting Young tableaux and non-intersecting paths via determinantal formulas.

Exact solvability of the dimer model

Speaker: Terrence George, TIFR CAM, Bangalore

Introducing the dimer model and Kasteleyn’s determinant method on planar graphs.

On the depth query problem for convex bodies

Speaker: Purvi Gupta, IISc Bangalore

Convex hull membership, polytope queries, and the Hilbert metric in data structures.

Protecting quantum states…using inverting pulses

\ Speaker: Deepak Dhar, ICTS Bangalore

Optimizing pulse sequences to preserve qubits—super-Zeno effect in quantum control.

Inequivalence of the Ball and Polydisc in ℂ²

Studying the Bergman kernel to show the unit ball isn’t biholomorphic to a product disc.
